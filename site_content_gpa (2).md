**MODULE 1**
### Research & Market Validation

**Subtitle:** Find niches with proven demand before writing a single word.

Publishing without research is manufacturing without a customer. This module teaches the systematic approach to identifying niches on Amazon that have measurable, existing demand — and positioning your book to compete within them from day one.

**What you will learn:**

**Amazon BSR Analysis:** Best Seller Rank (BSR) is Amazon's real-time demand signal. You will learn to read BSR data across categories and subcategories to identify niches with consistent buyer activity — not just trending topics.

**Keyword Architecture for KDP:** Amazon's search algorithm weights the title field above all other metadata. This module covers how to identify the primary keyword that belongs in your title, the secondary keywords for your subtitle, and the seven keyword fields in your KDP metadata — each with a specific strategic purpose.

**Competitive Gap Analysis:** Identifying what existing books in your niche are not delivering — through systematic review analysis — gives you a content positioning advantage that no amount of keyword research alone can provide.

**Gemini Deep Research Integration:** You will use Gemini's Deep Research feature to synthesize market demand data, competitive landscape analysis and keyword opportunity mapping into a single research document before beginning any production work.

**Deliverable:** A validated book concept with confirmed market demand, primary keyword in the title, and a content brief ready for the AI writing phase.

---

**MODULE 2**
### AI-Assisted Content Creation

**Subtitle:** Produce full-length, publication-ready manuscripts using Claude and structured prompting.

The most significant barrier to building a large publishing catalog is production speed. This module eliminates that barrier by establishing a systematic AI writing workflow that produces consistent, high-quality output at scale.

**What you will learn:**

**The 10-Chapter, 3-Topic Structure:** The specific architecture that keeps AI-generated content focused, coherent and structured — avoiding the drift and repetition that occurs when prompts are too broad. Every book in this academy follows this structure.

**The Editorial Brief System:** Before any AI session begins, you produce a complete editorial brief: title with primary keyword, full chapter outline, target reader profile, core transformation the reader achieves, tone specification and the seven KDP keyword phrases. This document is the foundation of every production session.

**Epic and Extensive Prompting:** The specific prompting language that instructs Claude to write in full, developed paragraphs — not summaries, not bullet lists, not abbreviated content. The prompt methodology that produces 600+ words per topic, developed with concrete examples and practical depth.

**Quality Control Workflow:** After production, every manuscript goes through a pre-publication review using ChatGPT to identify any phrasing that reads as machine-generated, any cultural missteps for the target market, and any terminology inconsistencies. This step is non-negotiable for international publishing.

**Deliverable:** A complete, reviewed manuscript ready for formatting and cover production.

**MODULE 3**
### Cover Design & Professional Formatting

**Subtitle:** Produce covers that convert and interiors that meet Amazon's technical standards.

A book on Amazon competes visually at thumbnail size — approximately 160 pixels wide in search results. Cover design is not aesthetic preference; it is conversion optimization. This module covers the complete cover-to-publication workflow.

**What you will learn:**

**The Visual Code of Your Niche:** Every successful niche on Amazon has an established visual language — dominant colors, typography conventions, iconography patterns. Readers recognize these signals unconsciously. Before designing any cover, you analyze the top 10 best-sellers in your niche and document the visual patterns.

**AI Cover Production Workflow:** Generate the base visual in ChatGPT, add title typography and layout in Canva (widescreen format), then validate and finalize in the Amazon KDP Cover Editor. This three-stage workflow consistently produces covers that meet Amazon's technical requirements while maintaining visual competitiveness.

**Internal Images with Nanobanana:** For illustrated content, children's books and books requiring consistent character design across multiple scenes, Nanobanana provides the visual consistency that general-purpose AI image tools cannot maintain across a full manuscript.

**Kindle Create for Interior Formatting:** Amazon's official formatting tool converts your DOCX manuscript to Kindle format. You will learn to use Kindle Create's preview function to verify every chapter break, heading hierarchy and list format across multiple device types before submission.

**Print Interior Standards:** Black and white interiors are the standard for print-on-demand through KDP. Color printing costs are structurally incompatible with competitive pricing in most nonfiction and fiction categories. This module covers the specific margin, bleed and spine width settings for professional print production.

**Deliverable:** A professionally formatted ebook file and print-ready interior, plus a validated cover that meets all Amazon KDP technical specifications.

**MODULE 4**
### Global KDP Logistics & Distribution

**Subtitle:** Navigate Amazon's publishing infrastructure, metadata strategy and international distribution.

Publishing a book on Amazon is not uploading a file. It is configuring a product listing for a global search engine. Every metadata field has a strategic function. This module covers the complete publication workflow from account setup to live listing.

**What you will learn:**

**KDP Account Configuration:** Complete setup including payment configuration for international authors. The module covers the specific payment infrastructure (Wise and Grey virtual accounts) that allows authors outside the United States to receive royalties via direct deposit — avoiding wire transfer fees and check processing delays.

**Metadata Architecture:** Title field optimization (primary keyword placement), subtitle construction (secondary keyword plus benefit statement), HTML-formatted description (hook, problem, learning outcomes, audience, CTA), seven keyword fields (3-to-5-word buyer intent phrases, no single keywords, no title repetition), and two-category selection strategy.

**KDP Select and Kindle Unlimited:** The strategic case for enrolling every new title in KDP Select — the 90-day exclusivity program that includes your book in Kindle Unlimited, provides Amazon's algorithm with a signal of author commitment, and gives you access to promotional tools including Kindle Countdown Deals and Free Book Promotions.

**Pages Read as a Performance Signal:** KDP Select enrollment provides access to KENP (Kindle Edition Normalized Pages) data — the number of pages read by Kindle Unlimited subscribers. This data is a direct quality signal: high pages-read-per-sale ratios indicate the content is retaining readers; low ratios indicate a gap between the cover/description promise and the content delivery.

**Publication Sequence:** Ebook first, always. The ebook validates demand, accumulates reviews and establishes the listing's search history. Print version follows once the ebook demonstrates consistent sales. Both formats share the same product page, concentrating all reviews and sales history in one place.

**Deliverable:** A fully published, live Amazon KDP listing with complete metadata, enrolled in KDP Select, with payment infrastructure configured for international royalty receipt.

The module covers the complete API workflow including prompt engineering for translation, quality verification and pre-publication review.

The specific questions to ask of this data: which titles are being read (vs. just purchased), which countries are generating disproportionate sales (translation opportunity signals), and which titles have declining BSR despite stable metadata (optimization candidates).

**Production Rhythm:** The operational reality of experienced publishers — the workflow that allows 2 to 3 titles per day with approximately 4 hours per title. The first book takes days. ## FAQ SECTION

**Section Label:** Frequently Asked Questions

**H2:**
Technical Questions, Direct Answers.

---

**Q1: Do I need writing experience to use this system?**

No. The academy teaches a structured prompting methodology that produces publication-ready manuscripts using AI. Your role in the writing process is editorial — providing the research foundation, the structural brief, and the quality review. The AI handles drafting. This is not a shortcut around quality; it is a production methodology that maintains quality at scale.

---

**Q2: How does Amazon KDP distribution actually work for authors outside the US?**

Amazon KDP distributes to every Amazon marketplace globally by default. When you publish, your book is listed on amazon.com, amazon.co.uk, amazon.de, amazon.es, amazon.fr, amazon.co.jp and others simultaneously. You set the price in each marketplace, and Amazon handles the local currency conversion, VAT compliance and customer service for each territory.

Royalties are calculated per marketplace and paid monthly with a 60-day delay. A sale in January is paid at the end of March. Royalties are paid in USD to your specified account.

---

**Q3: How do international authors receive royalties from Amazon KDP?**

Amazon KDP pays via direct deposit (ACH transfer) to a US bank account, or by wire transfer to international accounts. For authors in Kenya and across Africa, the practical solution is a virtual US bank account through Wise or Grey — both provide US routing and account numbers that Amazon treats as domestic payments. Module 4 covers this setup in complete detail.

---

**Q4: What royalty percentage does Amazon KDP pay?**

For ebooks priced between $2.99 and $9.99 USD, Amazon pays 70% of the list price minus a delivery fee (approximately $0.15 per megabyte of file size). Outside this price range, the royalty rate is 35%. For print books, the royalty is 60% of the list price minus the per-unit printing cost (which varies by page count and interior type). A 200-page black and white paperback has a printing cost of approximately $3.25, making a $9.99 print book viable at the 60% rate.

---

**Q5: What is KDP Select and should I enroll?**

KDP Select is Amazon's 90-day exclusivity program for ebooks. Enrolling means your ebook cannot be sold on any other platform during the enrollment period. In exchange, your book is included in the Kindle Unlimited subscription library, Amazon's algorithm gives your title increased visibility in recommendation systems, and you gain access to promotional tools including Kindle Countdown Deals.

The academy recommends enrolling every new title. The exclusivity cost is minimal because no competing platform approaches Amazon's global reach. The algorithmic benefits — particularly the increased visibility and Kindle Unlimited inclusion — consistently outweigh the exclusivity constraint for catalog-building authors.

---

**Q6: How does translation work, and which languages are most valuable?**

Translation via the DeepSeek API produces editorial-quality output — not sentence-by-sentence machine translation, but contextually aware, idiomatic translation that reads naturally in the target language. A pre-publication quality review using ChatGPT catches any remaining awkward phrasing or cultural missteps before the translated version is published.

Languages ranked by royalty opportunity for KDP authors: English (largest market, highest cover prices), Spanish (second-largest native-speaker market globally), German (European market with high purchasing power), French (global market, strong across multiple continents), Portuguese (Brazil plus Portugal).

---

**Q7: How long does it take to publish a first book?**

For a new student working through the full academy curriculum, the first book takes 7 to 14 days — including the research phase, full manuscript production, cover design, formatting and publication. After completing two or three books and internalizing the workflow, production time compresses significantly. Experienced publishers using this system produce 2 to 3 complete titles per day with approximately 4 hours per title.

---

**Q8: What is the difference between a low-content and high-content KDP book?**

High-content books are standard manuscripts — guides, non-fiction, fiction, how-to books — typically 10,000 words or more. They require the full writing workflow covered in Module 2.

Low-content books are products with minimal written content: journals, planners, notebooks, activity books. They require interior design rather than writing, and use different production tools (Canva for interior design, specific KDP interior templates). Both categories are viable on Amazon KDP. The academy focuses primarily on high-content publishing but covers low-content strategy in the context of catalog diversification.
